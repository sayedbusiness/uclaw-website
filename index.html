Questions, Answered

What injured people ask us first

How much does a personal injury lawyer cost?+

Nothing up front — ever. The consultation is free and we work on contingency: our fee comes only out of the compensation we recover for you. If we don't win, you don't pay attorney fees.

How long do I have to file a claim in California?+

In most cases, two years from the date of injury. Claims against government entities can require notice within six months. Evidence disappears fast either way — call as soon as you can.

What is my case worth?+

It depends on your medical costs, lost income, future treatment, and pain and suffering. Our recoveries have reached $5 million, but no honest lawyer quotes a number before understanding your case — that's what the free consultation is for.

Should I talk to the insurance company first?+

No — and don't give a recorded statement. Adjusters are trained to minimize claims, and your words can be used to reduce your compensation. Let us do the talking; it's free to start.

Do you really speak my language?+

Yes. Our team serves clients in English, Spanish, Dari/Farsi, Hindi/Urdu, and Arabic — not through a phone translation service, but through the people actually handling your case.

What if I can't travel to your office?+

We come to you — hospital, home, or by video — anywhere in the Sacramento region, including Elk Grove, Roseville, Folsom, Citrus Heights, and Rancho Cordova.

What should I do right after a car accident?+

Get medical care first, even if you feel fine — some injuries appear days later. Photograph the scene and vehicles, collect the other driver's information, request a police report, and talk to a lawyer before any insurance company.

How long does a personal injury case take?+

Straightforward claims often resolve in months; serious-injury cases can take a year or more, because settling before your medical picture is clear usually means settling for less. We move every case as fast as its full value allows.
